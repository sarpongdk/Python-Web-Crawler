Basic but functional

4/5
Stayed Feb 2019
greg s.dallas,tx

​Second time to stay at this location and we use it for the location. It's expensive for what you get. Right on bio bay, across the road from the beach & snorkeling (we see turtles, stingrays, squid, parrot fish and much more) and a 15 minute drive to see the unique Blue Iguanas​ (habitat tour 10am & 11am daily)

We know the place so we know to stock up on food, we plan our trips to the West side carefully and we always enjoy ourselves on Cayman.

The condo itself is basic, but functional and comfy. Wifi is okay, nice atrium to sit outside, we cook & kitchen is functional (knifes need a sharpen). Nice to have cleaning & paper products provided, only one key for the door is a pain, wonderful shared back area to grill and sit out by the bay, kayak provided and we used it to visit Kaibo and the mangroves as well as bio bay at night. However, at night time, the townhomes along the bay have a pool and why those lights are on past dark - nobody ever swims after dark. Then into our 2nd week, they fix the light on the boat dock - that adds another few lumens of light pollution that shines into your eyes & into the condo. So much for sitting outside enjoying the evening breeze and checking out the stars

Seeing notes around the condo to 'save the earth, conserve water' is fine, but if you invest in a shiny TV why not update the toilet to a lo flow version? It'll cost less than the TV.

Rum Point itself now has the "Death Star" of condos being built and speaking with some locals development across the island seem to be going crazy.

Cayman Kai is a community of beach front homes with a couple of quiet condo complexes. There are a few restaurants in the area and a water-sports operator located on Rum Point Beach, but it is generally a much quieter experience. This area is about 45 minutes drive from the airport, car hire is recommended if you choose to stay in this area.
